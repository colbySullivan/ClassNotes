Agile
	1970 William Royce introduced the Waterfall methodology
	In 2001 Jeff Sutherland, Ken Schwaber, and 15 others drafted the agile manifesto
	Agile Four Pillars
		Individuals and interactions over processes and tools
		Working software over comprehensive docs
		Customer collaboration over contract negotiation
		Responding to change over following a plan
	Flexible production framework
		Allows teams to adapt
		Ideal for exploration of new IP
		Allows teams to find the fun while working on a fixed schedule
		Informs stakeholders
		Teams can self-organize and stay motivated
	Development methodology based on iterative and incremental steps
	
Scrum - A framework for managing projects based on agile principles
	Work in small pieces at a time
	Team continually experiments and receives feedback
	Is a project philosophy or framework that takes an iterative approach
	Scrum is a type of Agile methodology
	Empirical Process control
		Used for complex processes that are not completely understood
		3 Pillars
			Transparency
			Inspection
			Adaptation
	Sprints
		Timeboxed 1-3 weeks
		Planning has two parts: Sprint goal and planning how to achieve the goal
	Scrum Team
		Development Team
		Scrum Master
		Product Owner
	Product Backlog
		List of desired functionality
		Maintained by Product owner
		Kept in priority order
		Items can be removed, added, prioritized
